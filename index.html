A little about me . . .

I'm currently a 5th year Ph.D. student in the Brain, Behavior, and Cognition program at Boston University and supported by the NINDS F31 fellowship. I work in the lab of Chandramouli Chandrasekaran researching how decision-making unfolds in the prefrontal cortex of rhesus macaques.

I expect to graduate early in 2026 but am starting early and exploring potential postdocs working at the intersection of cell types and decision-making. I'm especially interested in labs that leverage both electrophysiology and molecular techniques in order to study neural dynamics.

Blogging Philosophy

In keeping with the utility of a personal science website, I will mostly use this as a place to self-advertise. However, one thing I've come to realize is that the process of doing science is extremely different than it might appear on social media with every grant, single digit percentiled; every person, on an NIH fellowship; and every paper, appearing from thin air, fully-realized.


My name is Kenji Lee and I'm currently a Ph.D. student doing computational and cognitive neuroscience in the department of Psychological and Brain Sciences at Boston University. Previously I was a Research Associate at the Allen Institute for Brain Science; I received my M.S. in Applied Mathematics at the University Washington; and I obtained my B.S. in Biochemistry and Math with a minor in Neuroscience at the University of Puget Sound.
